DIAGNOSIS:
You hurt your low back. The muscles are sore.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Your exam looked safe today. You can walk. We did not see danger signs.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Move a little each day. Do not stay in bed all day.
- Use ice for 1 to 2 days. Then try heat if it helps.
- Do not lift heavy things for a few days. Do more as you feel better.
- Use pillows to get comfortable when you sleep.

MEDICATIONS:
- You can take acetaminophen for pain. Follow the bottle.
- Ibuprofen may help pain. Take it with food. Do not use it if a doctor told you not to.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now if you cannot control pee or poop. Come back if your private area feels numb.
- Come back now for weak legs, fever, a new injury, or much worse pain.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with a low back strain. I need a follow-up visit within 1-2 weeks if pain is not improving."
