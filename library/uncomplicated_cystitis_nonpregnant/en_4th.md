DIAGNOSIS:
You have a bladder infection, also called a UTI.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Today we did not find signs of a kidney infection or a dangerous infection in your blood.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Drink fluids if you are allowed to. Avoid alcohol or drinks that make burning worse until you feel better.
- If a urine culture was sent, the hospital may call you if your antibiotic needs to change.

MEDICATIONS:
- Take the antibiotic exactly as prescribed. Finish it unless a doctor or pharmacist tells you to stop.
- If you were given medicine for burning, use it only as directed. It may turn your urine bright orange.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Get checked again if burning or urgency is not starting to improve after 2 days of antibiotics, or if symptoms get worse.
- Come back now for fever, chills, back or side pain, vomiting, weakness, confusion, or feeling very sick.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with a urinary tract infection. I need a follow-up visit within 1 week, or sooner if symptoms are not improving."
