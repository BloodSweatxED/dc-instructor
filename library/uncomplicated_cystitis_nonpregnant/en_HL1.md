DIAGNOSIS:
You have a bladder infection, called a UTI.

WHAT WE FOUND:
We did not see signs of a kidney infection or dangerous infection today.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Drink fluids if you can. Avoid drinks that make burning worse.
- If a urine culture was sent, we may call if your medicine needs to change.

MEDICATIONS:
- Take your antibiotic as told. Finish it unless a doctor tells you to stop.
- If you got medicine for burning, take it as told. Your pee may turn orange.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Get checked if symptoms are not better after 2 days of antibiotics, or if they get worse.
- Come back now for fever, chills, back pain, vomiting, confusion, or feeling very sick.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with a urinary tract infection. I need a follow-up visit within 1 week, or sooner if symptoms are not improving."
