DIAGNOSIS:
You sprained your ankle. The x-ray did not show a broken bone.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Blood flow and feeling are good. Use the brace or crutches as told.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Use ice for 15 to 20 minutes. Keep your ankle raised.
- Wear the brace as told. Rest your ankle.
- Walk only as told. Use crutches if walking hurts a lot.

MEDICATIONS:
- You can take acetaminophen for pain. Follow the bottle.
- Ibuprofen may help pain and swelling. Take it with food. Do not use it if a doctor told you not to.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now if your toes are numb, cold, blue, pale, or hard to move.
- Come back now for much worse pain, worse swelling, fever, or if you cannot walk.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with an ankle sprain. I need a follow-up visit within 5-7 days if pain or walking is not improving."
