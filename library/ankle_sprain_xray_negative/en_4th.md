DIAGNOSIS:
You have an ankle sprain. Your x-ray did not show a broken bone.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Your foot has good blood flow and feeling. You can go home with a brace, wrap, or crutches as instructed.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Put ice on your ankle for 15 to 20 minutes at a time. Keep your ankle raised when you sit or lie down.
- Wear the brace or wrap as instructed. Rest your ankle for the next few days.
- Walk only as much as you were told to. If walking hurts a lot, use crutches and put less weight on the ankle.

MEDICATIONS:
- You can use acetaminophen for pain. Follow the bottle directions. Do not take more than the label says.
- Ibuprofen or naproxen may help pain and swelling. Take them with food. Do not use them if you have kidney disease, stomach bleeding, take blood thinners, or were told to avoid them.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now if your foot or toes become numb, cold, blue, very pale, or hard to move.
- Come back now if pain or swelling gets much worse, you cannot walk at all, the skin gets very red or hot, or you get a fever.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with an ankle sprain. I need a follow-up visit within 5-7 days if pain or walking is not improving."
