DIAGNOSIS:
Your symptoms fit uncomplicated triceps tendinitis.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Your clinician diagnosed triceps tendinitis. We did not find tendon rupture, infection, neurovascular compromise, or another emergency pathway today.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Rest the sore area as instructed.
- Use ice, gentle motion, activity limits, or gradual return to activity only as your clinician recommended.
- Avoid activity that causes sharp or worsening pain until you are improving.

MEDICATIONS:
- Use pain medicine only if prescribed or recommended, and follow the label.
- Do not combine medicines unless your clinician said it is safe.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Worsening severe pain, new swelling, numbness, weakness, color change, or trouble using the arm.
- A pop with loss of strength, new deformity, or concern for tendon rupture.
- Fever, spreading redness, warmth, drainage, or symptoms not improving as expected.

FOLLOW UP:
Follow up with primary care, sports medicine, orthopedics, urgent care, or the ED if symptoms are not improving or your clinician told you to recheck.
