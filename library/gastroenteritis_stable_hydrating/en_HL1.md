DIAGNOSIS:
You likely have a stomach virus. It can cause vomiting or diarrhea.

WHAT WE FOUND:
You were safe to go home today. Keep drinking fluids.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Take small sips often. Use water, broth, or rehydration drink.
- Eat bland foods when ready. Wash your hands well.

MEDICATIONS:
- If you got nausea medicine, take it as told so you can drink.
- Do not use diarrhea-stopping medicine with blood, high fever, or bad belly pain unless told.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now for bad belly pain, blood in stool, black stool, fainting, confusion, or dehydration.
- Come back if you cannot drink, pee much less, have high fever, or keep getting worse.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with gastroenteritis. I need a follow-up visit within 1-3 days if symptoms are not improving."
