DIAGNOSIS:
You most likely have gastroenteritis, a stomach and intestine infection that can cause vomiting or diarrhea.

WHAT WE FOUND:
You were stable enough to go home today. You can keep working on drinking fluids at home.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Take small sips often. Water, oral rehydration solution, broth, or a sports drink can help replace fluid.
- When you feel ready, start with bland foods. Wash your hands well because stomach infections can spread.

MEDICATIONS:
- If you were prescribed nausea medicine, take it only as directed so you can keep fluids down.
- Do not use diarrhea-stopping medicine if you have bloody stool, high fever, or severe belly pain unless a doctor tells you to.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now for severe belly pain, bloody or black stool, fainting, confusion, or signs of dehydration.
- Come back if you cannot keep fluids down, stop peeing normally, fever gets high, or symptoms keep getting worse.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with gastroenteritis. I need a follow-up visit within 1-3 days if symptoms are not improving."
