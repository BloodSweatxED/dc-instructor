DIAGNOSIS:
You have a viral upper respiratory infection, also called a cold. We do not see signs of pneumonia today.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Your breathing and oxygen level were safe for going home today.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Drink fluids, rest, and use a humidifier or steam from a shower if it helps congestion.
- Wash your hands often. Cover coughs. Stay away from people at high risk while you have fever or feel very sick.

MEDICATIONS:
- Antibiotics do not treat cold viruses. They were not needed based on today's visit.
- You can use acetaminophen or ibuprofen for fever or aches if these are safe for you. Read labels so you do not double up on the same medicine.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now for trouble breathing, chest pain, blue lips, confusion, fainting, or if you feel much worse.
- Come back if fever or cough gets better and then comes back worse, if fever stays high, or if you cannot drink enough fluids.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with a viral upper respiratory infection. I need a follow-up visit within 3-7 days if symptoms are not improving."
