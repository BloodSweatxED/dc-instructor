DIAGNOSIS:
You have a cold virus. We did not see pneumonia today.

WHAT WE FOUND:
Your breathing and oxygen level looked safe today.

WHAT TO DO AT HOME:
- Drink fluids and rest. Steam or a humidifier may help.
- Wash your hands. Cover coughs. Avoid high-risk people while you feel very sick.

MEDICATIONS:
- Antibiotics do not treat cold viruses. You do not need them today.
- Use fever medicine only if it is safe for you. Read labels. Do not double up.

RETURN TO ED IF:
- Come back now for trouble breathing, chest pain, blue lips, confusion, fainting, or feeling much worse.
- Come back if fever or cough gets better then worse, or if you cannot drink fluids.

FOLLOW UP:
Call your primary care doctor's office or clinic. Say, "I was in the emergency department and was diagnosed with a viral upper respiratory infection. I need a follow-up visit within 3-7 days if symptoms are not improving."
