# Phase 161 Acute Otitis Media Clinician Review Answers

Status: complete.

Decision: revise and keep draft. Do not promote.

Review answers:

1. Diagnosis wording is acceptable if it says middle ear infection and acute otitis media together.
2. Scope should be adult-only for now.
3. Antibiotic language should not imply automatic antibiotics. It should defer to clinician-entered medication instructions, including delayed-antibiotic or watchful-waiting plans.
4. Blockers should include mastoiditis, deep ear infection, perforation, tympanostomy tube, otitis externa, ear trauma, foreign body, acute hearing loss, vertigo, facial weakness, severe systemic illness, immunocompromise, recurrent or chronic ear infection, pediatric pathway, unstable vitals, and specialist-directed ENT plan.
5. Follow-up timing should not be hard-coded. Use the schedule the clinician gave.

Decision:

Revise `acute_otitis_media_uncomplicated` and keep it draft-only.
